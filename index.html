Appendix Table J. Interpretative Synthesis of Territorial Patterns
Territorial pattern Empirical indication Analytical interpretation
Concentration in Europe and North America Most studies are located in Europe and North America The field remains territorially concentrated in regions with stronger scientific infrastructure and institutionalized sustainability agendas.
Limited representation of Africa and Asia-Pacific Only residual presence of studies in Africa and Asia-Pacific The literature still shows asymmetric spatial coverage, with underrepresentation of important socio-territorial contexts.
Predominance of national and subnational approaches Most studies are anchored at national or subnational/regional levels Research tends to privilege institutionally bounded or policy-related territorial units rather than broader relational scales.
Presence of global but weakly localized studies A subset of studies is coded as global or supranational Part of the literature adopts broad sustainability discourses without strong territorial specification.
Residual unspecified territorial anchoring Some studies do not clearly define a territorial locus This suggests abstraction or weak territorial embeddedness in part of the field’s scientific production.
Source: Author’s elaboration based on the coded territorial anchoring of the bibliographic corpus.
